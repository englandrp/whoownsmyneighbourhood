3 June 2011

 

Congratulations to conductor Ed Gardner and mezzo soprano Sarah Connolly who have won plaudits galore for their new Britten recording with the BBC Symphony Orchestra for Chandos.

View profiles
Sarah Connolly
Edward Gardner

The disc features music that spans the composer's entire career ranging from an early student work 'Two Portraits'  and the 'Sinfonietta', the composer's Op. 1, to the song cycle 'A Charm of Lullabies' and 'Phaedra', the dramatic cantata written for Dame Janet Baker at the end of Britten's life. 
     
Writing in Gramophone Richard Fairman praised Gardner's 'sensitivity', calling his interpretation of the 'Sinfonietta' a 'brilliant performance'.  Of 'Phaedra' Fairman writes that 'spurred on by Gardner's keen sense of theatre, Sarah Connolly goes straight for the drama [using] her voice and breadth of scale to create a veritable operatic scena'.  Nigel Simeone, in the International Record Review considers Connolly to be  'tremendous in this new recording…her diction is impeccable and her sense of dramatic involvement is enormously impressive'

Bravo!
